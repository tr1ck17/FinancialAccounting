Which one of the following is not a current liability?

B. Wage expense

	Answer: Wage Expense

Multistep income statement
For example:
What’s the amount of Sales Revenue for Company A with the following data?
Cost of Goods Sold $2,240,000
Operating Expenses 560,000
Sales Discounts 40,000
Sales Returns and Allowances 260,000
Net Income 680,000

	Answer: We need to trace the steps backwards and find the Sales Revenue.

	Net Income + Operating Expenses = Gross Profit
	680,000 + 560,000 = 1,240,000

	Gross Profit + COGS = Net Sales
	1,240,000 + 2,240,000 = 3,480,000

	Net Sales + Sales Discounts + Sales Returns and Allowances = Sales Revenue
	3,480,000 + 40,000 + 260,000 = 3,780,000

	Sales Revenue = 3,780,000

Current ratio
For example:
A company has net income of $5,000, current assets of $10,000, total assets of $40,000, and
current liabilities of $8,000. What is the company’s current ratio?

	Answer:

	Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
	10,000 / 8,000 = 1.25

Return on sales ratio
For example:
A company has net income of $5,000, net sales of $20,000, and total assets of $50,000. What is
the company’s return on sales ratio?

	Answer:

	Return on Sales = Net Income / Net Sales
	5,000 / 20,000 = 0.25
	Return on Sales = 0.25

Working with the Statement of Stockholder’s Equity
For example:
Company D earns a net income of $430,500 and pays dividends of $39,000 during the period.
Company D has ending retained earnings of $733,500. What’s the balance in Company D’s
beginning retained earnings?

	Answer:

	Beginning Retained Earnings + Net Income - Dividends = Ending Retained Earnings
	x + 430,500 - 39,000 = 733,500
	733,500 - 430,500 + 39,000 = x
	Beginning Retained Earnings = 342,000

Working with the Statement of Cash Flows
For example:
The following amounts have been taken from the recent financial statements for Company E:
Current liabilities	Cash from operations 	Expenditures on PPE	Dividends (cash)
$1,780,000 		$8,400,000 		$3,000,000 		$2,600,000
What’s the amount of the free cash flow for Company E?

	Answer:

	Free Cash Flow = Net Cash Provided by Operating Activities - Capital Expenditures - Cash Dividends
	x = 8,400,000 - 3,000,000 - 2,600,000
	Free Cash Flow = 2,800,000
