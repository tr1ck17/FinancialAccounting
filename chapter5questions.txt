Cost flows
For example:
Company F purchases $180,000 of inventory during the period and sells $54,000 of it for
$90,000. Beginning of the period inventory was $9,000. What is the company’s inventory
balance to be reported on its balance sheet at year end?

	Answer:

	Beginning Inventory + Purchases - Cost of Goods Sold = Ending Inventory
	9,000 + 180,000 - 54,000 = Ending Inventory
	Ending Inventory = 135,000	(the 90,000 sales revenue is a distraction here)

Gross profit percentage
For example:
Company H reports net sales of $5,000, cost of goods sold of $3,000, and net income of $500.
What is the gross profit percentage for the company?

	Answer:

	To find gross profit:
	Net Sales - COGS = Gross Profit
	5,000 - 3,000 = 2,000
	Gross Profit Percentage = Gross Profit / Net Sales
		= 2,000 / 5,000
		= 40%
	($500 is a distractor used for the "Profit Margin" ratio, not Gross Profit)
