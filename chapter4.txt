Understanding Financial Statements

Assets:
	Current Assets (will be consumer or turned into cash within one year):
		Cash
		Short-term marketable securities
		Accounts receivable
		Other current assets
	Non-Current Assets
		Property, plant, and equipment
		Intangible assets
		Other long-term assets

Liabilities:
	Current Liabilities (will require payment within one year):
		Accounts payable
		Accrued expenses payable
		Short-term notes payable
		Other current liabilities
	Long-Term Liabilities
		Long-term notes payable
		Other long-term liabilities

Stockholders' Equity
	Contributed Capital
	Retained Earnings

Current Assets
	Assets that are expected to be converted into cash or consumed within the company's normal operating cycle or one year, whichever is longer
	Listed in order of expected liquidity, which is the ability of an asset to be readily converted into cash
		Cash
		Short-term investments
		Accounts receivable
		Inventory 
		Other current assets
Long-Term Assets
	Assets that the company does not expect to convert into cash during the normal operating cycle, or one year, whichever is longer
		Property, plant, and equipment
		Intangible assets
		Other long-term assets

Property, Plant, & Equipment (PP&E)
	Assets with a physical presence
		Land
		Buildings
		Equipment
		Vehicles
		Furnishings
Intangible Assets
	Assets that lack a physical presence
		Brand names
		Copyrights
		Patents
		Trademarks

Current Liabilities
	Obligations that must be settled within the normal operating cycle or one year, whichever is longer
	Listed in order of maturity
		Accounts Payable
		Accrued Expense
		Short-term notes payable
		Other current liabilities
Long-Term Liabilities
	Obligations that are not due to be settled within the normal operating cycle, or one year, whichever is less
		Long-term notes payable
		Bonds payable
Stockholders' Equity
	The residual ownership interest in the assets of a business after its liabilities have been settles
	Two major categories:
		Common stock
			Amount invested by stockholders
		Retained Earnings
			Cumulative net income of the business that has not been distributed to stockholders

Balance Sheet Presentation
	Which of the following is not an item on a classified balance sheet?
	Current Liabilities
	Long-term assets
	Sales revenue
	Retained earnings
	Answer: Sales Revenue

Single-Step Income Statement
	Subtracts the sum of expenses from the sum of revenues to calculate net income

Multi-Step Income Statement
	Presents revenues and expenses in various categories and calculates several subtotal performance measures before net income
		format differs somewhat for merchandising firms and services firms
	Facilitates financial analysis and management decision-making

Net sales = Total sales - sales returns and allowances - sales discounts

Which of the following is not a component of a multi-step income statement
	Operating expenses
	Net sales
	Income from operations
	Current assets
	Answer: Current Assets

Ratio Analysis
	Relation of one accounting number to another through the process of division
		Expressed as a percentage, a rate, or a proportion
	Used to evaluate operating performance and financial health of a company
	An example: Return on Assets (ROA) = Net income / Total assets
	Techniques:
		Trend analysis
			compares same company over time
		Benchmarking
			compares company to peer firms

Working with the Balance Sheet
	Identifies how assets are funded
		debt
		equity (common stock and retained earnings)
	Evaluate the firm's financial health
		liquidity: refer to a company's ability to pay its short-term financial obligation. It depends on several factors, including the level of cash a company has and how quickly it can generate cash from operations or its assets

		solvency: refer to a company's ability to pay its long-term financial obligation

Liquidity
	Ability to pay short-term obligations from cash and current assets
	Current ratio = Current assets / current liabilities
	Current sufficient current assets to pay its current obligations

Solvency
	Ability to pay obligations over the long-term
	Debt-to-Total Assets ratio = Total liabilities / Total assets

Working with the Income Statement
	Profitability Analysis
		Ability to generate profit from the firm's operations
		Return on sales ratio = net income / net sales
		Higher ratio indicates a more profitable company
		Helps in comparing different size firms

Working with the Statement of Stockholder's Equity
	The statement of stockholder's equity consists of two parts:
		contributed capital
			capital contributed by the stockholders of the company
		earned capital
			capital earned by the company, reinvested in the business, and not distributed to the stockholders - that is, its retained earnings

Working with the Statement of Cash Flows
	Company's cash inflows (sources) and cash outflows (uses) over a period
	Categorized by type of business activity:
		operating, investing, financing
	Free cash flow = 
		cash flow from operations - Capital Expenditures for PP&E
			cash flow available after maintaining current level of capital investment

Which of the following involves an analysis of the income statement?
	Current ratio
	Return on sales ratio
	Debt-to-total assets ratio
	Free cash flow
	Answer: Return on Sales ratio
